MUSIC AND DANCE

Traditional Dances

MOKHIBO:

Mokhibo is a woman dance performed on the knees, the body gently rising and falling as the hands are swept upwards.

It is performed by a women. There's a group of choir that stands at the backline that sing for the dances.

1.MOHOBELO:

Mohobelo dance is an energetic traditonal dance exclusively performed by Basotho men in Lesotho

Originating among warrios in the 1800s, it features rhythmic stomping, high leaps and coordinated movements.

The dance is a powerful celebration of Basotho culture, history and masculinity

2.NTLAMO:

Ntlamo dance is a Basotho traditional dance for men and boys, characterized by vigorous foot-stamping,

clapping and high kicks often perforned in line formations during public events and ceremonies like weedings.

3.LITOLOBONYA:

Litolobonya is a traditional Basotho dance song and dance practice performed by both girls and married woman, with distinct versions for each group.

The girls dance is done in the open for everyone to see, while the version for married women is secretive ritual performed during the celebratory pitiki gathering that marks the birth of a child

Istruments

1.LESIBA:

Is a mouthresonated bow with a flattened quill attached to a string that vibrates when the player blows across it

2.LEKOLULO:

A traditional fluete that is primarily played by herding boys

SETOLO-TOLO:

An extended jaw harp that is played by men who use their mouth to produce sound.

4.THOMO:

A stringed instrument traditionally played by women in Basotho culture.

5.SEKHANKULA:

A more recent addition to basotho music, this single-stringed, bowed monochord instrument features a tin can resonator and played by friction with a bow.

6.KORIANA:

A popular instrument in the Famo music genre which originated in the early 20th century among migrant mine workers.
